Privacy Policy

Last updated: July 2026

Parrot is designed around a simple principle: your child's practice data is your family's business, not ours.

What Parrot collects

Nothing. Parrot does not create an account, does not require sign-in, and does not transmit word banks, session results, or accuracy scores to any server. Everything is stored locally in the app's on-device database.

Children's information

Parrot is designed for use by a parent alongside their child. Any kid profiles, word lists, or session data added to Parrot are stored only within the app's local storage on your device and are never uploaded or shared with any third party.

Analytics and advertising

Parrot contains no analytics SDKs, no advertising SDKs, and no third-party trackers of any kind.

Data deletion

Deleting the Parrot app from your device permanently removes all of its data. There is no cloud backup to separately delete.

Children's privacy (COPPA)

Parrot does not collect personal information from children because it does not collect any information at all — everything stays on-device, and the app is intended to be used by children under parental supervision.

Changes to this policy

If this policy changes, the update will be posted on this page with a new effective date.

Contact
